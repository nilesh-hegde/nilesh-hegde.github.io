Hi!

I am a recent graduate with a Masters in Computer Science (specializing in AI) from the Khoury College of Computer Sciences (Northeastern University). I am broadly interested in natural language processing, Data Science and applications of machine learning to the computational sciences.

My research interest focuses on computational social science, machine learning optimization and algorithmic graph theory. My goal is to design practical systems as well as understand the underlying human intelligence behind them. Recently I have been exploring the topics Bayesian Parametrics and Decision Theory.

I was fortunate enough to be guided and mentored by so many great reseachers and I deeply appreciate their help. I was part of Prof. Bruce Maxwell's research group at Northeastern while working on color constancy and Prof. Suresh M Hegde's Graph Theory and Combinatorics group at NIT Surathkal while working on graph labeling.

I graduated with a B.Sc.(Hons) in Computer Science and Engineering from B.M.S. College of Engineering, Bengaluru, India.

I am currently a Research Assistant at the Center for Inclusive Computing, where I work with Prof. Felix Muzny and Prof. Carla Brodley.
